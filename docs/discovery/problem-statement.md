# Problem Statement

## Core problem

Students may avoid reporting possible bullying situations because they fear retaliation, being identified, or making the situation worse.

At the same time, schools need to assess the information they receive, document their decisions, coordinate actions, and follow up on possible cases in a secure and traceable way.

## Why it matters

When a possible bullying situation is not reported, the affected student may remain without support and the situation may continue or become more serious.

When information reaches the school, an incomplete or fragmented management process can make assessment, coordination, documentation, and long-term follow-up more difficult.

## Key domain distinctions

- The person submitting a report is not necessarily the possible victim. They may be a classmate, witness, or another person who knows about the situation.
- A report contains information that still needs to be assessed. It is not proof that bullying has occurred.
- A report and an internal case are different concepts. An internal case may be opened after the school reviews the report and decides that investigation or formal management is necessary.
